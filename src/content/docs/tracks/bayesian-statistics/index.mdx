This track develops Bayesian reasoning from the ground up — beginning with the derivation of Bayes' rule and progressing through conjugate models to full Bayesian linear regression. Each note builds on the last, with dense cross-links to keep the knowledge graph tight.

## What You'll Learn

- How Bayes' rule inverts conditional probabilities to update beliefs
- Conjugate prior families and why they produce tractable closed-form posteriors
- How to extract point estimates and credible intervals from posterior distributions
- The connection between Bayesian regression, ridge regression, and OLS
- How prediction uncertainty grows with extrapolation and why

## Prerequisites

- Basic probability (conditional probability, independence)
- Familiarity with Gaussian distributions is helpful but not required

Each note is self-contained but designed to be read in order. The derivations are shown explicitly inside collapsible sections — they are optional but highly recommended for building real intuition.
